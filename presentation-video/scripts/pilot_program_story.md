# Pilot Program Story

## Pilot Positioning

The Ministry has already invited a detailed presentation. The presentation should not try to prove that digital health is exciting. It should show that this Abuja pilot is small enough to approve, practical enough to implement, useful enough to measure, and scalable only after evidence.

## Pilot Scope

- 2 to 3 selected Primary Healthcare Centres
- 1 General Hospital referral pathway
- Participating doctors
- Participating pharmacy partners
- Ministry/Department oversight dashboard
- Monthly reporting
- 3-month active launch window inside a broader 12-month implementation plan

## Patient Journey

1. Amina Yusuf requests care at a PHC or through DoctaRx.
2. Nurse Grace confirms basic intake and captures the request.
3. Dr. Musa Ibrahim reviews the case and consults remotely where appropriate.
4. The doctor issues a prescription or referral.
5. Pharmacist Ada receives prescription visibility where applicable.
6. Mr. Bello receives the referral summary and updates follow-up status.
7. The Ministry dashboard shows aggregate pilot indicators, not private patient-level information.

## Why This Is Low Risk

- It does not replace existing PHCs, hospitals, doctors, pharmacies, or government systems.
- It starts with selected facilities and controlled workflows.
- It uses synthetic presentation data for demonstration.
- It measures adoption, referral completion, follow-up visibility, response time, and reporting usefulness before any scale decision.
