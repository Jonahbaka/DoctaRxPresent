# Female Voice Narration Script

Voice direction: calm female healthcare narrator, warm and reassuring, clear English, professional public-health explainer tone, suitable for a Ministry healthcare presentation.

In Abuja, the first win does not need to be complicated.

The proposed DoctaRx pilot starts small: selected primary healthcare centres, one general hospital, participating doctors, pharmacy partners, and a Ministry oversight dashboard.

A patient request can be captured at a PHC or through DoctaRx. Nurse Grace confirms the basic intake, so the next health worker sees the right context.

Dr. Musa reviews the case and consults remotely where appropriate. He can document the encounter, issue a prescription, or create a referral for hospital follow-up.

Pharmacist Ada sees the prescription workflow where applicable. Mr. Bello, the referral officer, sees the referral summary and follow-up status.

The Ministry does not need patient-level private information to understand the pilot. It needs aggregate visibility: requests, consultations, referrals, prescriptions, completion status, response time, follow-up visibility, and staff feedback.

The pilot is low risk because it does not replace existing hospitals, PHCs, doctors, pharmacies, or government systems. It simply adds coordination around the patient journey.

Start small. Measure results. Improve the workflow. Scale only after evidence.

This is the care journey DoctaRx proposes to strengthen. Jonah Baka will now present the pilot roadmap and partnership details.
